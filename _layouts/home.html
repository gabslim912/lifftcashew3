LIFFT-Cashew Project

Linking Infrastructure, Finance and Farms to Cashew

This is a 6-year program (2017-2023) funded by USDA to enhance the production and trade of cashews in local and international markets. The target areas are the cashew growing regions of Senegal, The Gambia and Guinea-Bissau.

The project works around three objectives which are:
  • Production of RCN: Increase or maintain quality of RCN outturn in 52-54 lbs;
  • Sales of RCN: Strengthen associations /cooperatives and sale up to 50% of RCN produced through them by organizing collective sales;
  • Processing of RCN: Increase processing of RCN to additional 30000 tons of RCN locally.

what we do

These objectives are implemented by four components working in perfect synergy

Infrastructure

More than 130 kilometers of roads built

Access to finance

Facilitating private investment and loans between loans and the cashew sector

Market Access

Organizing collective sales and commercialization

On-Farm Practices

Improving the quality of cashews and introducing organic certification

The integration of the three governments (Senegal, The Gambia and Guinea Bissau) remains one of the major priorities for the sustainability of the activities implemented. Read more..

COVID-19 impact on the cashew value chain

In the first few months of a normal year, over 55% of all cashews would be harvested by farmers in West Africa, dried and shipped in raw form to Asian processors before being sold to consumers worldwide. But this year, the pandemic was hitting the sector just as West Africa’s cashew marketing season was meant to kick off.
